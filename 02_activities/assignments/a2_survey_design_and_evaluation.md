#  SCENARIO 3: University of Toronto - Music Taste and Age

# Purpose of Survey:
```
This survey investigates the relationship between age and music taste, examining perceptions of popular music across age groups and how preferences change over a lifetime. Findings will contribute to sociological understanding of cultural consumption and generational identity, intended for academic publication.
```

# Target Population, Sampling Frame, Units, and Strategy:
```
Target Population: Adults aged 18-80 in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA) who listen to 
music at least weekly.

Sampling Frame: 
(1) University of Toronto community database (students, staff, faculty - ~90,000 individuals), and 
(2) Community recruitment through libraries, senior centers, and community boards for age diversity beyond university.

Sampling Units : Individual adults aged 18-80 in the GTA.

Sampling Strategy: Stratified random sampling with age as primary stratification variable. 
Six age strata: 18-25, 26-35, 36-45, 46-55, 56-65, 66-80. 
Disproportionate allocation oversampling older groups (typically underrepresented). 
Target: 600 total (100 per stratum). 

survey method :
We will run the survey mainly online, but we'll also offer a paper version to make it easier for older people to take part. Because we might get more responses from some age groups than others, we will use statistics to adjust the final results. This will make sure our findings accurately reflect the true age mix of the Greater Toronto Area

Justification:
 We separated people into different age groups because age is the main thing we're studying. We made sure to include enough older people so we could make fair and reliable comparisons between all the age groups. We used different ways to find participants to make sure our survey included all kinds of people. Finally, we used statistical weights to make sure our final results accurately represent the entire population.

 ```

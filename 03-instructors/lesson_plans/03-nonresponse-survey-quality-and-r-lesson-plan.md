# Module 5: Sampling

## Lesson 3: Nonresponse and Survey Quality

#### Estimated Time: 5 hours

#### Concepts:
- Nonresponse bias
- Weighting
- Total survey quality
- Measurement error
- Coverage error

#### Lesson Description:
This lesson considers elements of survey quality, error, and bias resulting from how a survey is conducted and designed. It also introduces students to survey analysis in R for the first time.

#### Instructor Preparation:
- Read over ACS materials
- Set up Slido poll
- Read over 2011 CES documentation

### Learning Goals
- Identify causes of and remedies for nonresponse
- Assess survey quality through total survey error

## Lesson Content & Instructor Notes

### 08-Nonresponse

- **Introduction (15 minutes)**
  - Review previous day’s material
- **Lesson (45 minutes)**
  - 08-nonresponse-slides
- **Activity / Discussion (60 minutes)**
  - Have students identify and summarize methods for dealing with nonresponse in the American Community Survey. Consider response rates, nonresponse bias, and weighting methods.
    - [American Community Survey Design and Methodology (January 2014) Chapter 11: Weighting and Estimation](https://www2.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/methodology/design_and_methodology/acs_design_methodology_ch11_2014.pdf)

> **Instructor Notes**: 
    > - Ask students to name and describe sampling strategies discussed in Lesson 2 (02-populations-censuses-surveys-and-observational-data-slides). 
    > - Discuss usual methodology first, then COVID-19 adjustments. Optional: Give half of the class the usual methodology documentation and give the other half the COVID-19 overview. Ask for short summaries of each.
